Is Nostalgia Coloring Our Memory?
Back in 1976, we did not have the internet. We were still on landline.
Today, we have myriad digital devices such as iPads and cells phones to go along with the 
internet. We can order online, we can stream shows, we can search for information at our
fingertips. And now, we can look forward to all of the wonders (or doom?) that AI can bring
us.
So, let's compare the 250th anniversary against the 200th anniversary in 1976. Was 1976
really better days or is nostalgia fooling us? 
Since we can do a comparison on so many facets, I'm going to focus on a few selected items:
unemployment, inflation and weather.
Let's see what the data tells us.
Index of tabs:
Unemployment: compares 1976 and 2026 unemployment rate, both at the national level
and at state level. At the state level you can select the state you want to look at.
Unemployment Insights: CoPilot created this tab to provide the trends and insights it can
